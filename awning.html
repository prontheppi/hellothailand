Awning Windows

Awning windows are designed to open outwards from the bottom. This unique design allows for continuous airflow even on rainy days, providing superior ventilation while maintaining your privacy.

Please contact us

Key Features & Benefits

  • Rain Protection: The outward-opening design acts like a small awning, shielding the opening from rain and allowing you to ventilate your space even during a drizzle.
  • Continuous Airflow: You can leave the window open for passive ventilation throughout the day, improving indoor air quality without worrying about the weather.
  • Privacy and Security: Ideal for high-wall installations in spaces like bathrooms or basements, allowing natural light and airflow without compromising your privacy.
  • Energy Efficiency: When closed, the tight compression seal creates an airtight fit, preventing air leakage and improving your home's insulation.

Considerations

  • Exterior Cleaning: The sash cannot be flipped inwards, which may make it more challenging to clean the outside of the glass.
  • Limited View: The opened sash can obstruct the view and they are not suitable for emergency exits.

Usage & Application Guide

  • Bathrooms & Kitchens: An excellent choice for rooms that need both airflow and privacy.
  • Combined with Other Windows: Awning windows are often installed above or below other fixed windows to provide ventilation without disrupting the main view.
  • Basement Windows: Their high-placement and tight seal make them a secure option for basements or utility rooms.
